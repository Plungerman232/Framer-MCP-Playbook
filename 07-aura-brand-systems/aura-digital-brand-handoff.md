# Aura Digital Brand Handoff

## 1. Chat Title / Topic

Aura Digital — Web Design / Digital Experiences Page Handoff

## 2. Main Build Goal

Create a dedicated Aura Digital page for the Aura brand. Aura Digital is the web development / web design / digital experiences branch of the larger Aura Creative umbrella brand.

The goal is to make Aura Digital feel cohesive with Aura Creative and Aura Visual while still having its own identity as the “digital architecture” branch of the brand. It should not feel like a random generic agency page, SaaS template, or basic web design portfolio.

Aura Digital should feel like:

> The digital wing of the Aura universe — websites, interfaces, and branded digital experiences built with atmosphere, intention, and identity.

---

## 3. Aura Creative Umbrella Brand Direction

Aura Creative is the main umbrella brand. It has a dark cinematic cosmic/storm aesthetic with purple, magenta, black, and subtle red tones. The brand feels premium, mysterious, atmospheric, polished, editorial, and creative.

The overall feeling is:

- entering a world
- stepping into a portal
- cinematic brand atmosphere
- premium creative studio energy
- mysterious but professional
- expressive without becoming gimmicky

The umbrella homepage established the strongest visual system so far and should be treated as the primary source of truth.

---

## 4. Three-Branch Brand Structure

Aura has three main branches:

1. **Aura Visual**
   - Photography
   - Visual storytelling
   - Editorial imagery
   - Portraits, graduation, brand, lifestyle, automotive, studio work

2. **Aura Digital**
   - Web design
   - Web development
   - Framer websites
   - Landing pages
   - Digital experiences
   - Brand-aligned web systems

3. **Aura Creative**
   - Creative direction
   - Strategic consulting
   - Brand concepts
   - Creative systems
   - Overall umbrella / direction layer

Aura Digital should be treated as one wing of the larger Aura Creative world.

---

## 5. Established Visual Identity

The Aura visual identity includes:

- dark cosmic storm atmosphere
- black / charcoal base
- deep purple
- magenta
- subtle red
- lightning / nebula / portal energy
- atmospheric glow
- premium spacing
- custom image-based cards/buttons
- cinematic footer text
- elegant serif typography
- small uppercase nav/button typography
- thin borders
- subtle hover effects
- strong center compositions
- a “portal into a world” feeling

Aura should not feel like a normal template site.

---

## 6. Typography Style

The typography direction is one of the strongest parts of the brand.

Use:

- large elegant serif typography for major titles
- wide letter spacing
- small uppercase navigation
- small uppercase buttons
- thin, premium labels
- minimal body copy
- cinematic spacing

The umbrella page typography should guide Aura Digital.

Aura Digital title treatment should feel similar to:

```text
A U R A   D I G I T A L
WEB DESIGN & DIGITAL EXPERIENCES
```

The type should feel fashion/editorial, not corporate SaaS.

---

## 7. Logo / Emblem Direction

Aura has a custom logo/emblem direction involving a symbolic mark with subtle arcs and a small figure/energy motif.

For sub-pages like Aura Visual, Aura Digital, and Aura Creative, use the Aura logo/emblem in the top-left corner instead of plain text whenever possible.

The umbrella homepage can use plain text when cleaner, but the sub-pages should use the logo to reinforce brand cohesion.

---

- Digital

It works because the cards feel like portals into each branch, not just normal buttons.

Aura Digital should carry this same system forward with its own set of custom cards.

## 9. What Aura Visual Taught Us

Aura Visual helped clarify how sub-pages should work.

Important lesson:

> Sub-pages should not become separate unrelated websites. They should feel like wings inside the same Aura universe.

For Aura Visual, the direction became:

- use the Aura logo top-left
- keep dark premium atmosphere
- keep custom cards/buttons
- use real work/photos as proof, but do not let them overpower the identity
- avoid generic photography website layouts
- make the page feel like “photography through the Aura lens”

That same lesson applies directly to Aura Digital.

Aura Digital should not look like a generic web agency page. It should feel like “web design through the Aura lens.”

Aura Digital should feel more:

- structured
- precise
- architectural
- interface-focused
- futuristic
- immersive

Aura Visual is photography/editorial.

Aura Digital should feel like digital spaces, web portals, interfaces, systems, and online experiences.

Visual metaphors that fit Aura Digital:

- glowing portal
- vertical digital monolith
- floating interface panels
- dark web architecture
- browser-window fragments inside a storm
- digital grid fading into cosmic landscape

- subtle wireframes

- web experience as a “world”

Avoid making it too literal with random laptops.

## 23. Agent-Ready Summary

Aura Digital is the web design / digital experiences branch of the Aura Creative brand. It must feel like a cohesive part of the Aura universe, not a generic agency page. The Aura universe is dark, cinematic, cosmic, storm-like, premium, and editorial, with black, purple, magenta, and subtle red tones. Typography should be elegant, serif-based, widely spaced, and paired with small uppercase nav/button text.

Aura Digital should carry over the Aura logo, dark atmosphere, custom card/button system, premium spacing, footer text, and portal-like feeling from the umbrella homepage. It should differ by leaning more technological, precise, architectural, and interface-driven. The core visual metaphor should be digital portals, web architecture, and interface systems inside the Aura storm.

Recommended page structure: Aura logo/nav, cinematic hero, AURA DIGITAL title, “Web Design & Digital Experiences” subtitle, concise premium copy, CTAs, three custom cards for Design / Build / Launch, concise service section, simple process section, work/examples, final CTA, and minimal footer.

The page should make visitors feel that Aura Digital builds websites with taste, atmosphere, and strategy — digital spaces that feel like worlds.
